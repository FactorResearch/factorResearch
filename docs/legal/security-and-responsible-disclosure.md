# Security and Responsible Disclosure

Security reports help us protect users and the integrity of Cenvarn.

## Reporting a vulnerability

Send reports to **[security@cenvarn.com]**. Include:

- A description of the issue.
- Affected URL, endpoint, or feature.
- Reproduction steps.
- Potential impact.
- Screenshots or proof-of-concept material that does not expose personal information.
- Your preferred contact information.

Do not send sensitive credentials or personal information in the initial report.

## Good-faith research

We ask researchers to:

- Avoid privacy violations, service disruption, denial of service, spam, social engineering, physical attacks, and destructive testing.
- Test only accounts and data you own or are authorized to use.
- Stop and report promptly if personal information is encountered.
- Avoid downloading, modifying, or deleting user data.
- Give us reasonable time to investigate before public disclosure.
- Comply with applicable law.

This page is not authorization to access third-party providers, financial-data vendors, hosting platforms, or other systems outside our control.

## Our response

We aim to acknowledge valid reports, investigate based on severity, keep reporters informed where practical, and remediate confirmed issues according to risk.

Do not promise fixed response times or a bug bounty until the operational capacity and budget exist.

## Security incidents

Users who suspect account compromise should reset their password, revoke active sessions where available, and contact [support@cenvarn.com].
