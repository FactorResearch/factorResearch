Cenvarn methodology

Scoring Methodology

Close

Transparent. Auditable. Deterministic. Every Cenvarn score can be traced to publicly available financial data and a documented mathematical formula.

Philosophy

Transparency: Every score, rating, and recommendation can be explained. Reproducibility: Investors should be able to reproduce results from the same SEC filings and documented formulas. Independence: Models rely primarily on company fundamentals rather than analyst opinions. Evidence-based investing: Models draw from established frameworks, academic research, or historically validated quantitative methodologies.

Data Sources

Fundamental financial data comes directly from the SEC EDGAR XBRL filing system, including income statements, balance sheets, cash flow statements, shares outstanding, filing dates, and XBRL facts. Market data is retrieved in priority order from Finnhub, Tiingo, and Alpha Vantage.

What We Do Not Use

Cenvarn does not use black-box AI scoring, undocumented proprietary formulas, paid analyst opinions as part of the composite score, hidden weighting adjustments, or manual overrides.

Scoring Models

The methodology covers Composite Score, Graham, Buffett, Piotroski F-Score, Altman Z-Score, Quality, Momentum, Risk Metrics, Profitability, Free Cash Flow Quality, Capital Allocation, Growth Quality, Earnings Revision, Factor Momentum, Greenblatt Magic Formula, Regime Overlay, Insider Activity, Alternative Data, Data Lineage, and Reproducibility.

Each model documents its purpose, data required, scoring criteria, mathematical formula, interpretation, and limitations. The methodology is updated alongside the implementation as the platform evolves.
