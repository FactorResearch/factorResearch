# Security and Privacy Incident

1. Declare SEV-1 for confirmed cross-user access, active compromise, credential exposure with use, or regulated-data exfiltration; preserve UTC evidence and custody.
2. Contain by revoking credentials/sessions, isolating affected roles or services, and disabling unsafe writes without destroying logs or attacker artifacts.
3. Determine affected identities, fields, systems, vendors, jurisdictions, and earliest/latest exposure using minimal-access queries.
4. Rotate secrets at their authority, redeploy clean artifacts, validate authorization boundaries, and monitor for continued indicators.
5. Security/privacy/legal owns regulator, customer, insurer, and law-enforcement decisions and deadlines; record the decision even when notification is not required.
6. Recover only after exploit paths are closed, old credentials fail, clean synthetic journeys pass, and heightened monitoring is active.
7. Preserve a sanitized timeline and complete root-cause/corrective-action review without placing sensitive evidence in the repository.
